03 · The research loop

Every answer creates
three more questions.

  1. 01

    Search

    Start with a need, then translate it into keywords a store might understand.

  2. 02

    Open

    Scan retailer pages, editorial lists, reviews, and discussions in parallel.

  3. 03

    Compare

    Reconcile different names, missing details, stale prices, and conflicting claims.

  4. 04

    Repeat

    Discover a new tradeoff and begin the same fragmented process all over again.

The burden of synthesis sits with the shopper—not the shopping experience.

06 · Comparison overload

More options do not create
a better decision.

Choice paralysis

Endless near-identical options increase hesitation without increasing understanding.

False equivalence

Spec tables make every feature look equally important, even when only a few shape the outcome.

Hidden tradeoffs

The cheapest, highest-rated, or most popular option can still be wrong for a person’s priorities.

The real job

Reduce the decision to the differences that genuinely matter.

07 · Generic recommendations

The right product depends
on the person.

What listings know

  • Category and price
  • Ratings and review count
  • Features and specifications
  • Popularity and promotion

What people actually mean

  • “It has to fit my small apartment.”
  • “I care more about comfort than power.”
  • “This is a gift and I cannot get it wrong.”
  • “I will pay more if it lasts.”

Relevance begins where filters stop: with situation, priorities, and compromise.
